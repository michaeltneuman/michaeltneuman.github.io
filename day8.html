Day 8: More Sheep

When traveling a lot, I get really really weird dreams. This morning I woke up after having a dream that I got into a car accident and got arrested for a DUI. My dad talked to the Glenview (my hometown) Police Department and they agreed to drop the charges if I gave a lesson on math at the public library. On the way there, my dad got into a car accident with like 8 bicyclists. The last thing I remember is going up in front of a thousand high schoolers with no idea what to say. I woke up covered in sweat and then thought about my dream for two seconds and laughed. Traveling definitely puts extra stress on both your body and mind because you're out of your regular element.

After my relaxing night of sleep, we departed for our next destination: Killarney. When looking on the map, we found a shitton of goofy sounding town names. This one was my personal favorite. On the drive to there, we saw a massive amount of sheep. My sister told me this ridiculous story about how in Hawaii in the 1990s there was a hurricane. At the time, there were only a few chicken farms on the island of Kaua'i. After this hurricane came through, the chickens were displaced all over the island so now there's just chickens everywhere. This story had nothing to do with the trip but I thought it was worth sharing.

Once we arrived to Killarney, we immediately headed to the Gap of Dunloe for a hike. We were debating whether to rent bikes for the ride up and thank goodness we didn't becuase it was STEEP. There were plenty of sheep, rams and horses to see along the walk. I had a little misunderstanding with one on the road. I walked up kind of close and taunted him and he started to get his feet ready to run at me. Thankfully, he just kind of snubbed me and walked on by. I definitely did not taunt any more sheep after that. Concluding our hike, we ate some dinner and saw a man perform some live Irish music. There were even people up and dancing during dinnertime, so silly.
