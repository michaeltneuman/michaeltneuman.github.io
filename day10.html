Day 10: Cliffs of Moher

On the ferry ride across the river, there were supposedly doplhins that surfaced near the front of the boat momentarily before hiding back beneath the ocean waves. I was not at the front of the boat so I didn't see the dolphins. FOMD or Fear of Missing Dolphins is a serious problem in Ireland and I got it bad.

When we were walking along the Cliffs of Moher, I overheard this overly enthusiastic Irish man telling people "TO TRULY EXPERIENCE THE CLIFFS, YOU MUST JOURNEY BEYOND THIS FENCE" pointed at a fence that was clearly designed for people to NOT walk behind (safety reasons).

We saw a 6 month old sheepdog being trained. The trainer was shouting commands at the little guy "left! right! sit! up! left! left!" while the dog listened to almost every command and herded these sheep. It was fascinating. The sheep were even trained to be herded I think. Or they at least had stamps on them, basically the same thing.

In the evening, we finally arrived in Galway at this super fancy hotel which you could easily get lost in. In fact, while walking around, I heard an 80 year old Irish woman scream "How the FUCK do you get out OF HERE? HONEY!" before awkwardly staring at me.
